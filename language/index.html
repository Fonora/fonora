Fonoran

Communicate by constructing meaning, not memorizing it.

Fonoran is an experimental language built from a small set of shared semantic roots. Instead of memorizing thousands of words, speakers combine concepts to create new meaning.

Our research asks a simple question:

Can two people with no shared native language learn enough of this system to communicate after only a short period of study?

A tiered language

Fonoran grows in three rings. Each ring adds reach while keeping the same compositional approach of combining shared roots.

  • Communicative core

    ~50 roots

    The experiment: how far can two strangers communicate with only fifty?

  • Extended core

    ~100 roots

    Everyday fluency — ordinary conversation while preserving the same root-combination strategy.

  • Complete language

    Open-ended

    Specialized and abstract vocabulary, admitted only when simpler combinations repeatedly prove insufficient.

Imagine two strangers stranded together with no common language. Could this root realistically come up during their first week of interaction?

Ordered by human experience

Roots are grouped the way a person experiences the world — not by linguistic category. Bodily and spatial concepts come before social and emotional ones; abstraction is admitted last.

Experience Examples
Survival & body person, eat, drink, sleep, pain, sick, hot, cold, see, hear, hand, eye, mouth
Space & motion up, down, inside, outside, near, far, water, fire, earth, sky, tree, animal
Social friend, family, help, share, give, take, work, leader, group
Emotion love, fear, happy, sad, angry, calm, trust, hope, lonely, proud
Time before, after, now, yesterday, tomorrow, always, never
Thinking know, think, remember, forget, understand, learn, question
Abstract equal, cause, change, value, system — only when repeatedly proven necessary
