Fonora

An open research project exploring language from first principles.

Fonora is an open-source research project exploring how writing systems and languages can be designed from first principles. It combines experimental software, a phonetic script, and a constructed language to test new ideas about communication.

Our hypothesis: Can a language designed from first principles be learned quickly enough that two people with no shared native language can achieve practical communication after only a short period of study?

Project Goals

  • Explore new approaches to written and spoken language
  • Design a highly learnable writing system
  • Design a highly learnable constructed language
  • Reduce memorization through phonetic writing and semantic composition
  • Test communication between people with different native languages
  • Build open-source tools for language experimentation

Experimental Research Project

Fonora is an active open-source research project exploring how written and spoken language can be designed from first principles.

The project includes a phonetic writing system, an experimental constructed language, and the software used to build, analyze, and test both.

Every symbol, sound mapping, root, and grammar rule is treated as a hypothesis rather than a conclusion. Ideas are refined through experimentation, measurement, and community feedback.

The central research question is simple:

Can a writing system and language designed from first principles be learned quickly enough that two people with no shared native language can achieve practical communication after only a short period of study?

    Fonora Script
    
                
    A phonetic writing system constructed from speech itself.
    
              
    
            
    
          
    
          
    
            
    
              Fonora is an open-source, experimental writing system that represents speech using a small set of symbols based on how sounds are physically produced in the mouth.
            
    
            
    
              Rather than preserving historical spelling conventions, Fonora writes pronunciation directly. Each symbol represents either where a sound is produced or how it is modified, allowing readers to infer pronunciation from the written form itself.
            
    
            
    
              The project explores whether a compact, highly learnable script can represent speech across many languages while remaining visually simple and consistent.

    Vowels
    
        
    Simple vowels are ⚬X (2 symbols). Diphthongs are ⚬XᵔY (4 symbols): vowel indicator ⚬, a place or manner glyph, approximant ᵔ, and a destination place.

    Fonora Samples
    
        
    Speech-based writing across languages
    
      
    

      
    
        Fonora is designed to represent speech, not spelling. These samples show how paragraph-length text from different languages may appear when rendered through the Fonora phonetic system.
      
    
      
    
        The goal is to visually demonstrate how Fonora can represent speech across languages and dialects using the same symbol inventory regardless of the source writing system.
      
    

      
    
        Fonora is experimental. Samples may change as the phonetic mapping system improves. Listen uses neural voices and reads recovered Fonora phonetics. Japanese audio is not available yet.
      
    

      
    

      
    
        These samples are for visual and phonetic testing only. Fonora mappings may evolve over time. Excerpts are from the Universal Declaration of Human Rights, Article 1 (public domain).
